AMM Volatility Harvesting

Two simultaneous NSE market orders simulate each AMM rebalance. Buy leg + sell leg = cash arbitrage profit. All quantities are whole shares only. Fractional dust is discarded. Recentering incurs a real rebalancing trade at full brokerage cost.

Capital & Brokerage

0.15% buy + 0.15% sell = 0.30% round-trip (Zerodha/NSE standard). Capital is split 50/50 by value, floored to whole shares. Leftover cash from rounding is not deployed.

🛑 IL Stop-Loss

Halts all swapping once pool asset value drops more than this% below the buy-and-hold value. Set to 0 to disable.

Concentration Range Widths

Narrower = more profit per swap, higher IL risk.
Wider = safer inventory, fewer profitable swaps.

Dynamic Correlation & Recentering

Impact 0.6: width grows by 60% when corr = 0 (fully uncorrelated).
Trigger 75%: recenter when drift ≥ 75% of current half-width. Each recenter executes a real market rebalancing trade.

Volume Regime Detector

Hourly volume vs rolling mean ± σ·σ classifies LOW / MID / HIGH.

How each swap works (real NSE execution)

1
Price ratio shifts

Asset1/Asset2 price ratio moves. AMM equation determines new required inventory at the new price within the concentration range.

2
BUY the underweighted stock (whole shares)

Place a market buy order on NSE. Quantity = ⌊AMM delta⌋ (integer). Cost = shares × market price. Buy brokerage deducted.

3
SELL the overweighted stock (whole shares)

AMM releases ⌊delta⌋ whole shares. Market sell order placed simultaneously. Revenue = shares × market price. Sell brokerage deducted.

4
Net profit banked only if > 0

Gross = revenue − cost. Net = gross − buy brokerage − sell brokerage. Trade aborted if net ≤ 0. Fractional share dust is discarded.
