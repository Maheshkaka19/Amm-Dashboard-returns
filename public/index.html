NSE Stock Pool · Concentrated Liquidity

AMM Volatility Harvesting

Simulates a stock-to-stock swap pool on Indian markets. Because stocks can't be transferred between demats instantly, each rebalance places simultaneous buy + sell orders: buy the cheap leg from the open market, release the expensive leg via the AMM equation, sell it back. All profit is cash. Brokerage (0.3% on total notional) is deducted per swap.

Capital & Brokerage

Capital is split 50/50 by value between both stocks at open.
Brokerage is deducted from gross profit on every swap. A swap only executes if net profit > 0.

🛑 IL Stop-Loss

If the pool's asset value drops more than this % below the buy-and-hold value, all swapping halts immediately for the rest of the backtest period. Set to 0 to disable.

Concentration Range Widths

Narrower = more profitable per swap but higher IL risk.
Wider = lower profit per swap but safer inventory drift.

Dynamic Correlation & Recentering

Correlation Impact 0.6 → width increases by 60% when corr = 0.
Recenter Trigger 75% → re-center when ratio drifts 75% of current width.

Volume Regime Detector

Each hour is classified LOW / MID / HIGH based on how many σ its volume deviates from the rolling mean.

How profits are calculated

1
AMM detects a price divergence

The price ratio of the two stocks shifts. The V3 AMM equation tells us how much of each stock the pool should now hold.

2
Buy the cheap leg from the market

We buy Δ units of the underweighted stock from the open market at current price. Cash out = Δ × price.

3
AMM releases the overweighted leg

The AMM equation gives us Δ′ units of the overweighted stock to release. We sell these at market price. Cash in = Δ′ × price.

4
Net profit = revenue − cost − brokerage

0.3% brokerage on total notional (both legs) is deducted. Trade only executes if net profit > 0.
