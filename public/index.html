Capital & Brokerage

0.15%+0.15% = 0.30% round-trip (Zerodha/NSE standard). Capital split 50/50 by value, rounded to nearest whole share.

🛑 IL Stop-Loss

Halts all swapping once pool asset value drops more than this% below hold value. 0 = disabled (recommended for backtesting).

Concentration Range Widths

Half-width of the active price-ratio band. Swaps execute only while ratio is within ±width% of the center.
Tighter = more fee capture, more recenters.
Wider = fewer recenters, lower fee capture.
Recommended starting point: LOW 0.5% / MID 1% / HIGH 2%.

Dynamic Correlation Management

Impact 0.6 → width grows by 60% when correlation = 0 (fully diverging).
Impact 0.0 → fixed width regardless of correlation.
High corr stocks (>0.8): use lower impact. Low corr pairs: use higher impact.

Volume Regime

Each hour classified as LOW/MID/HIGH by comparing its volume to rolling mean ± σ·SD. Drives which base width is used.

Strategy Guide — Net-Zero / Positive vs Hold

  • Use correlated pairs (sector peers, Nifty50 heavyweights) — lowers IL
  • Tight width (0.5–1%) captures more micro-moves per rupee deployed
  • Low brokerage — every recenter costs 0.30%; frequent recentering with wide band destroys profit
  • Correlation impact 0.4–0.8 — widens when pair diverges, preventing premature recenters
  • Disable IL stop-loss during backtesting — it can prematurely halt profitable periods
  • Recenter enabled — keeps pool active; disabled means liquidity is idle when out-of-range

How each arbitrage swap works (two simultaneous NSE orders)

1
Price ratio moves inside the active band

External p1/p2 diverges from pool's internal y/x ratio. Arbitrage opportunity exists. We act as pool operator capturing the spread.

2
Buy input asset — round-to-nearest whole shares

Market BUY order on NSE. Qty = round(AMM target delta). Cost = Qty × price. Buy brokerage 0.15% charged.

3
Pool releases output via x·y=k law

Output qty = round(y − k/(x+input)). Derived exactly from invariant — not approximated. Floor guard prevents over-selling.

4
Sell output, bank net profit

Market SELL order. Revenue = shares × price. Sell brokerage 0.15%. Net = revenue − cost − both brokerages. Only executes if net > 0.
