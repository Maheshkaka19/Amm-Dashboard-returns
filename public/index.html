🔬 Virtual Liquidity Depth

📐
What it does

Sets the depth of the constant-product pool (k = xVirtual × yVirtual). A deeper pool generates smaller, more frequent swap signals. A shallower pool produces chunkier, less frequent trades.

💡
Example

Real capital ₹20 L, Virtual ₹2 Cr → 10× depth. The pool behaves as if it holds 10× more shares, giving much tighter concentration and more frequent small swaps — all executed with your real ₹20 L.

⚖️
Trade-off

Higher virtual depth = more swaps = more alpha potential but also higher cumulative brokerage. Lower = fewer trades, less brokerage, less IL.
