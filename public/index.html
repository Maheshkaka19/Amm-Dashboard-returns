NSE Stock Pool · x·y = k · Whole Shares Only

AMM Volatility Harvesting

Runs a self-managed constant-product pool (x·y = k) on two NSE stocks. Each rebalance is simulated as two simultaneous market orders — buy the input leg, AMM releases the output leg, sell it. All quantities are whole shares. No fractions. Profits accumulate as cash. IL is tracked separately.

Capital & Brokerage

Capital split 50/50 by value, floored to whole shares. 0.15%+0.15% = 0.30% round-trip (Zerodha standard).

🛑 IL Stop-Loss

Halts all new swaps once pool asset value drops more than this % below hold value. Set 0 to disable.

Concentration Range

Half-width of the price-ratio band. Swaps only execute when the current ratio is within this % of the center. Narrower = more swaps per move, higher IL exposure.

Dynamic Correlation & Recentering

When price drifts outside the range, a real market rebalancing trade is placed (brokerage charged). If recentering is disabled, the pool simply pauses until price returns to the band.

Volume Regime

Classifies each hour as LOW/MID/HIGH by comparing volume to rolling mean ± σ·SD.

How each swap is executed (NSE market orders)

1
Price ratio shifts

External p1/p2 diverges from pool's internal y/x. This creates an arbitrage opportunity in the pool.

2
Buy input asset (whole shares) from NSE

Place market buy order. Qty = ⌊AMM delta⌋ integer shares. Cost = Qty × market price. Buy brokerage charged.

3
AMM releases output asset (x·y=k law)

Pool absorbs the input. Constant-product equation yields exact integer output shares. Pool inventory updated.

4
Sell output asset to NSE, bank net profit

Market sell order. Revenue = shares × price. Sell brokerage deducted. Net profit banked only if > 0.
